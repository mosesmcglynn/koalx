Taper Fade

A haircut where the hair gradually decreases in length from the top down to the neck, creating a fade effect.

Undercut

Features short sides and back, while the top remains longer, offering a distinct contrast.

Crew Cut

Short hair on the sides and back with a slightly longer, often textured, top.

Buzz Cut

A uniform, short-length haircut achieved with clippers, creating an even length all over the head.

Side Part

Hair is styled with a visible parting on one side, creating a classic and polished look.

Textured Crop

Shorter sides and back with slightly longer, textured hair on top for a modern and textured appearance.

Mushroom Cut

Features a rounded shape with slightly longer hair on top and shorter sides, resembling a mushroom.

Fade with Hard Part

A fade haircut complemented by a distinct shaved line (hard part) for a sharp look.

French Crop

Shorter sides and back with a cropped or textured top, offering a modern and easy-to-maintain style.

Long Top Short Sides

Characterized by longer hair on top contrasting with shorter sides, allowing for versatile styling options.

Edgar Cut

Short back and sides with a longer, slightly messy top, popular among boys in the Philippines.

Caesar Cut

Features short, horizontally straight-cut bangs, offering a classic and versatile look for boys.

Low Fade

The low fade starts lower down the head, typically around the ear or slightly above it. It gradually tapers the hair shorter as it reaches the neckline, providing a subtle and less dramatic transition between lengths.

High Fade

Contrarily, the high fade begins higher up the head, around the temples or even above them. This style creates a more pronounced contrast between the longer hair on top and the significantly shorter sides and back, offering a more dramatic and bold look.
